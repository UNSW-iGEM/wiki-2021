Preparation of Agarose Gel for Electrophoresis


  1. To prepare a 50mL agarose gel solution, first add 5g of agarose powder to an 100mL empty bottle before adding 50mL of 99% TAE buffer.
  2. Heat the solution in the microwave until it starts bubbling, take out and swirl until agarose powder dissolves and the solution turns clear. Repeat several times until the agarose powder has completely dissolved.
  3. Pour the agarose gel solution into a chamber with the comb set up and allow it to cool.
  4. Add 2μL of 6X dye to 10μL of DNA, mix it slightly before loading into wells.

Ampicillin Stock Preparation


  1. Label a container.
  2. Weigh 57.5 g of Ampicillin trihydrate powder in a container using an analytical balance.
  3. Add 1mL of water to the same container.
  4. Run the tube in a gravity centrifuge for a short period of time.
  5. Vortex contents of the container. A cloudy solution will appear.
  6. Gradually add 20 μL volumes of NaOH. Shake the container before adding the next 20 μL volume. Continue adding 20 μL until the solution starts to clear up. Then slow down by adding 5 μL volumes of NaOH shaking before addition of the next 5 μL volume of NaOH. When the solution is completely clear, stop adding NaOH. Further addition will result in a yellow solution which indicates that you need to repeat the procedure and throw away the current solution.
  7. Take a green filter and screw a syringe to it. Place the solution inside the syringe. Place the filter-syringe-solution on top of a labeled microfuge tube. Push the plunger firmly at the beginning until liquid comes out of the filter. Continue pushing the plunger until the final volume of the syringe is on the microfuge tube. Give a final push on the plunger until you see foaming of the solution on the end of the filter inside the microfuge tube.
  8. Place the microfuge tubes on the fridge for future use.

BugBuster Protein Extraction


  1. Harvest cells from liquid culture by centrifugation at 10,000 × g for 10 min using a weighed centrifuge tube. For small scale extractions (1.5 ml or less), centrifugation can be performed in a 1.5-ml tube at 14,000–16,000 × g. Decant and allow the pellet to drain, removing as much liquid as possible. Determine the wet weight of the pellet.
  2. Resuspend the cell pellet in room temperature BugBuster Master Mix by pipetting or gentle vortexing, using 5 ml reagent per gram of wet cell paste. This typically corresponds to about 2.5 ml per 50-ml. culture. For small cultures use up to 1/5 culture volume for resuspension (e.g., use 300 µl BugBuster® Master Mix for 1.5 ml cultures). There are no adverse effects to using larger volumes of BugBuster Master Mix.
  3. Optional: Add protease inhibitors. Protease inhibitors are compatible with BugBuster Master Mix.
  4. Incubate the cell suspension on a shaking platform or rotating mixer at a slow setting for 10–20 min at room temperature.
    Note: The extract should not be viscous after incubation.
  5. Remove insoluble cell debris by centrifugation at 16,000 × g for 20 min at 4°C. If desired, save the pellet for inclusion body purification.
  6. Transfer the supernatant to a fresh tube. For SDS-PAGE analysis, remove a small sample (25–50 μl) and combine with equal volume of 4X SDS Sample Buffer (Cat. No. 70607-3). Immediately heat for 3 min at 85°C to denature proteins and then store at –20°C until SDS-PAGE analysis.

E. Coli Growth Assay


  1. Prepare overnight culture of the control and target sample.
  2. From the overnight culture inoculate a day culture and allow it to grow up to 0.6 OD.
  3. Induce protein production in the day culture
  4. Once day culture has reached 0.6 OD dilute down to 0.1 OD (this will be what is assayed)
  5. Add 50 mL (?) tubes with LB broth, 50 μL of ampicillin and _ μL of the 0.1OD target strain
  6. Repeat 4. for the control.
  7. Incubate the 50 mL tubes of the control and target strain at 37° C.
  8. Measure the OD using the spectrometer and record once an hour for 6 hrs. Repeat 6 and 7 for 45° C.
  9. Plot the data for each experiment into growth curves.

E. Coli Survival Assay, Heat Shock


Materials
  • Overnight culture: 200μL
  • Day Culture: 20mL of LB, 200μL of culture, 20μL of AMP
  • IPTG: 10μL of 1mM
  • LB agar plates
  • Bugbuster Master Mix
  • SDS-Page
Procedure
    Inoculation of overnight cultures for day cultures
  1. Prepare an overnight culture of sHSP and mCerulean.
  2. Prepare day culture and incubate at 37℃ for 1-1.5 hours until it reaches an OD of 0.4. Prepare day culture of sHSP and mCerulean w/o IPTG after whatever time is required for protein induction.
  3. Day culture components 20mL of LB, 200uL of culture, 20uL of AMP.
  4. To measure OD transfer 800uL of solution to a cuvette, replace 800ul of LB into the day tubes.

    5. Induce Protein Expression
  5. Add IPTG to the day culture at OD 0.4
  6. 10 μL of IPTG into HSP22 w IPTG and mCER. w IPTG
  7. Incubate for 1 hr at 37℃ to until OD of 0.6
  8. Measure OD before heat shock.

    9. Heat Shock
  9. Set the PCR to a gradient of 37℃ to 61℃ 55℃ and set to run for 10 minutes, set the holding temp to 20°C and infinite holding time

  10. Plating
  11. Use LB agar plates with ampicillin added. Label the plates as below with a version for 10-5 and 10-6.
  12. HSP22E w IPTG
  13. HSP22E without IPTG
  14. mCer w IPTG
  15. mCer without IPTG
  16. Repeat the plates for each temperature.
  17. After the samples have run in the PCR machine perform the following dilutions:
    • From the Original tube take 1μL and add to 999μL of PSB+water, this = 10-3
    • From the 10-3 take 1.5 μL and add it to 148.5μL of PSB+water, this = 10-5
  18. Spread plate using the following method:
    • on the 10-5 plate add 100μL from the 10-5 tube
    • On the 10-6 plate add 90μL of PSB+water into the centre and add 10μL from the 10-5 tube. Mix in the centre with the spreader and spread as normal
  19. Incubate at 37℃ overnight
  20. View results the following day

    21. Protein Extraction
  21. Use Bugbuster Master Mix to extract protein.
    (See Bugbuster Master Mix protocol)

    22. Protein Separation: SDS-Page
  22. Perform SDS-page for protein separation.
  23. Incoulate the a diluted overnight culture to an OD 0.6 into a day culture.
  24. Induce protein production into the day culture.

LB broth and LB agar preparation


Materials
  • Mili-Q water 1L
  • Agar 20 g/L, 2%
  • NaCl 10 g/L, 1%
  • Tryptone 10 g/L, 1%
  • Yeast extract 5 g/L, 0.5%
Procedure
    LB Broth
  1. Label containers with relevant details
  2. Name, LB broth or agar, date
  3. Add the powder ingredients into an appropriately sized container.
  4. Ingredients for broth include: yeast, Tryptone, and NaCl. Do NOT add Agar.
  5. Once all dry ingredients are added, clean and pack up the station.
  6. Mix dry ingredients in a container to evenly distribute.
  7. Add in Mili-Q water to the container.
  8. While adding in the water, swirl the contents to help mix and distribute components.
  9. Add autoclave tape and send the completed mixture for autoclaving.

    10. LB Agar
  10. Label containers
  11. Repeat step 2. and add Agar
  12. Repeat steps 3 and 4.

Overnight Culture


  1. Place 5 mL of LB broth into a labelled tube.
  2. Place 5 μL of Ampicillin stock solution into the same tube.
  3. Inoculate a single colony of bacteria from an agar plate into the tube.
  4. Incubate at 37°C overnight in a shaking incubator.

Streaking for Single Colonies


  1. Prepare antibiotic selection agar plates and label the plates.
  2. Collect sample of interest.
  3. Sterilize a 20μL pipette using 70% ethanol and paper towel.
  4. Set up a bunsen burner for sterile inoculation.
  5. Using a 20μL pipette, scrape some cells from the sample.
  6. Inoculate bacterial cells from sample of interest onto the agar plate.
  7. Streak out the culture, swapping pipette tips between streaks to maintain sterility.
  8. Incubate at 37°C overnight.
